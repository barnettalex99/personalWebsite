What's With The Mushrooms?

Mycorrhizal fungi form a network that connects each other as well as other trees and plants. This network, referred to by some as the "Wood Wide Web," allow the plants and fungi to share nutrients as well as information in a vast, complicated information system. Much like these mushrooms, I hope to be able to use data networks and systems to reach a wide, diverse range of people and share resources and information that can better their lives. In my current research job, I am working with a team to access de-identified health data sets and analyze their data in order to help us answer the question: "Why are African-American mothers facing higher morbidity and mortality rates in the U.S.?" It is questions like these that inspire me, and I am so excited to be part of such a connected, data-rich world where we all have the power to contibute to the ecosystem and make positive change.
